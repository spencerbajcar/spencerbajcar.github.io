Plant Propagation: Tips for Success

As an enthusiastic plant parent, I've learned that propagating plants is both rewarding and cost-effective. Here are some key tips I've discovered:

  • Always use clean, sharp tools when taking cuttings to prevent damage and disease
  • For stem cuttings, cut just below a node (where leaves meet the stem)
  • Most plants can be propagated in water or well-draining soil
  • Maintain humidity by covering cuttings with a clear plastic bag
  • Be patient! Some plants take weeks to root while others may take months

My favorite plants to propagate are pothos, monstera, and snake plants. They're practically foolproof for beginners!
